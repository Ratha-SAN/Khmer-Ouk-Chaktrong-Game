ច្បាប់អុកចត្រង្គ — Rules of Ouk Chatrang

Pieces & moves

KhmerNameEnglishMove
ស្ដេចSdachKingOne square in any direction.
នាងNeangQueenOne square diagonally only.
គោលKoulBishopOne square diagonally, or one square straight forward.
សេះSesKnightExactly like a chess knight.
ទូកTuukBoat / RookExactly like a chess rook.
ត្រីTreyFish / PawnOne square forward; captures one square diagonally forward. No double step, no en passant.

Setup

Trey start on each side's third rank. The kings do not face each other: White King on e1, Black King on d8, each Neang opposite the enemy King. White moves first. There is no castling.

Special first moves

On its first move the Sdach may jump like a knight (never a capture, never while in check). On its first move the Neang may leap two squares straight forward (never a capture; it may pass over an occupied square).

Sources conflict on when these jumps are forfeited. Default here: both jumps are lost once any piece has been captured. Alternative (selectable in settings): the King's jump is permanently lost once an enemy Tuuk aligns on the King's rank or file — even if the Tuuk later moves away, and even if other pieces stand between.

Promotion

A Trey promotes immediately and mandatorily on reaching the sixth rank (the opponent's third), and thereafter moves as a Neang.

Game end

Checkmate wins. Stalemate is a draw.

Counting rules (draws for the weaker side)

Board's honor: a player with three or fewer pieces may start counting from 1 with a limit of 64; they may stop at any time, and a restart begins from 1 again.

Piece's honor: when no unpromoted Trey remain and a player has only the bare Sdach, that player may instead count starting from (all pieces on the board, both Sdach included) + 1, with the limit fixed by the stronger side's force — the smallest applicable value:

Stronger side has…Limit
Two Tuuk8
One Tuuk16
Two Koul22
Two Ses32
One Koul44
One Ses64
Only Neang / promoted Trey64

These limits are the commonly cited table; exact values vary slightly between Cambodian and Thai sources. The table lives in one editable constant (PIECE_HONOR_TABLE) in the engine script. Once piece-counting starts the limit never changes, even if pieces are captured later; stopping and restarting re-fixes it. If the count reaches the limit before checkmate, the game is a draw.
